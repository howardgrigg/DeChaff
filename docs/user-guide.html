User Guide

How to use DeChaff

A complete walkthrough of every step, setting, and shortcut.

1 Load 2 Trim 3 Info 4 Chapters 5 Output After processing Keyboard shortcuts Settings Tips
1
Step one

Load

Get your audio into DeChaff. Four ways to do it.

Drop or choose a file

Drag an audio file onto the drop zone, or click Choose File… Supported: WAV, MP3, M4A, AIFF, FLAC, CAF.

YouTube browser

Browse recent videos and live streams from your church's YouTube channel. Tap a video to download its audio. A progress bar shows status; Cancel aborts at any time. AI fills in metadata while the download runs.

Paste a URL

Switch to the YouTube URL tab, paste any URL supported by yt-dlp (YouTube, Vimeo, and thousands more), and click the download button.

Safari Share & Shortcuts

On any YouTube page, tap Safari's Share button and choose DeChaff — the app opens and starts downloading immediately. Or use the Download & Open in DeChaff action in the Shortcuts app, or say "Download sermon in DeChaff" to Siri.

AI metadata extraction

When loading from YouTube, DeChaff uses on-device Apple Intelligence to parse the video title into sermon title, Bible reading, preacher, and series — concurrently with the download. If Apple Intelligence isn't available it falls back to the Claude API (if configured), then to built-in text parsing. By the time you reach the Info step, the fields are already filled in.

To make extraction accurate for your church, open Settings → YouTube and set the YouTube title format your team uses (e.g. {title}, {reading} | {preacher} | {series}). The YouTube tab also shows which extraction tier is currently active.

2
Step two

Trim

Cut pre-service noise and dead air. Switch between a waveform view and a transcript view — both stay in sync.

Waveform view

  • Drag the orange handles directly on the waveform
  • Move the playhead and press I (in) or O (out)
  • Click Set In / Set Out in the toolbar
  • Press ⌘Z to undo any move

Transcript view

  • Switch to the Transcript tab — DeChaff transcribes the audio on-device
  • Tap the first word you want to keep, then tap the last — the selection highlights in orange
  • Trim handles in the waveform view update instantly, and vice versa
  • Tap Clear to start the selection again

Playback

  • Click the waveform to jump to any position
  • Space or K — play / pause
  • J or ← — back 5 s
  • L or → — forward 5 s

Zoom & pan

  • Scroll vertically to zoom — anchored on cursor
  • Scroll horizontally or drag to pan
  • Double-tap to reset zoom to full view

Step three

Info

These fields are embedded in the MP3 as ID3 tags and used to name the output file.

FieldNotes
Sermon Titlee.g. The Saving Power of Jesus
Bible Readinge.g. Romans 1:16–17
PreacherRemembered between sessions
SeriesRemembered between sessions
DateDefaults to today; the full date prefixes the output filename
ArtworkDrag an image or click to choose. Remembered between sessions.

Output filename

Built from the tag fields using a customisable template. The default is:

YYYY-MM-DD Sermon Title, Bible Reading | Preacher | Series.mp3

Change the format in Settings → Templates. Saved to the same folder as the source file, or to ~/Downloads for YouTube downloads.

4
Step four

Chapters

Drop markers at key moments — podcast apps show these as a navigable chapter list.

Adding chapters

  • Scrub to the moment, then press M or click + Add Chapter
  • First chapter defaults to the start, labelled with the Bible reading
  • Second chapter defaults to 2 min in, labelled Sermon

Editing chapters

  • Drag a marker on the waveform to reposition
  • Click the time field and type a time (m:ss or h:mm:ss)
  • Click a title to rename it
  • Click − to delete; press ⌘Z to undo

Chapter times are in output time — relative to the trim-in point. A chapter at 0:00 plays at the very start of the trimmed recording.

5
Step five

Output

Review audio and output format options, then click Process or press ⌘↩. A transcript is generated automatically.

Audio processing

OptionDefaultWhat it does
Voice IsolationOnApple's engine removes background noise, room reverb, and crowd sounds.
Dynamic CompressionOnEvens out volume — quieter passages up, peaks controlled.
Long-Term LevellingOnSlow AGC that smooths volume differences across the recording (~3-second time constant, ±6 dB).
Loudness NormalisationOnTargets −16 LUFS (EBU R128) with a soft limiter to prevent clipping.
Mono OutputOnMixes to a single channel. Recommended — speech in stereo wastes file size.
Shorten Long SilencesOnTrims pauses longer than the threshold. Default maximum is 1.0 s.

Output format

OptionDefaultNotes
FormatMP3WAV produces a larger uncompressed file.
Bitrate128 kbps64 kbps is fine for speech; 128 kbps+ if music quality matters.

Done screen

Output file

Transcript

Full text transcript (if transcription was enabled) with a Copy button.

To process another file, click Process Another or drop a new file onto the window.

Tips

Getting the best results

A few habits that make the workflow faster.

Trim precisely

Zoom into the waveform at the start and end before placing handles. Use I/O keys while playing back, then press ⌘Z if you overshoot.

Shorten silences

The default 1.0 s works well for most sermons. Too short and delivery feels rushed; too long and pauses drag.

Chapter timing

If you're unsure where a section starts, play the recording and press M at the right moment. Drag the marker afterwards to fine-tune.

Preacher and Series persist

These are remembered between sessions — you only need to update them when they change.

Artwork persists too

Set it once and it will be embedded in every recording until you change it.

Use transcript trim for precision

If you know the first and last words of the sermon, switch to the Transcript tab and tap them — it's often faster than scrubbing the waveform to find the exact moment.

Set your title format once

If your church's YouTube titles follow a different convention, set it in Settings → YouTube and AI extraction will work correctly every time.
